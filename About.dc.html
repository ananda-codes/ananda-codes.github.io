About

Building and simulating the hardware behind modern computing.

I am a computer engineer with over nine years of combined experience in advanced computer architecture, with an emphasis on cache memory design, chiplet-based SoC architecture, and multi-fidelity system simulation.

My work centers on pre-RTL evaluation of hardware microarchitectures — enabling early estimation of power, performance, and area (PPA), architecture exploration, and hardware–software co-design through system modeling and simulation.

I have executed sponsored research for Boeing, Collins Aerospace, DARPA, and NSF, spanning chiplet SoC design, distributed AI, and embedded cybersecurity. I am experienced in handling multiple projects with competing priorities, and I am currently a PhD Candidate in Computer Engineering at Iowa State University.
